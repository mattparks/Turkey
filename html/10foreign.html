Foreign policy issues and initiatives / Relationship with the United States

Turkey is focused on becomming a EU member state, and looks to have more trade with countries. Turkey is already part of the EU's Common Market, and gets lots of trade with Europe. Turkey has poor relations with middle eastern countries like Syria and Iraq, hated by some as they are "to western".


Turkish relations opened in 1831 with the Ottoman Empire, and then with the Turkish Republic. Turkey has been a member to NATO since 1952, and have helped with the fight aganst ISIS, opening millitary bases to the United States. The US-Turkey trade was at $17.4 billion in 2015, and is expected to grow. The US exports aircraft, resources and food to Turkey, and has invested into its market.
