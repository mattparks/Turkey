Domestic policy + HDI, Gini

Turkey is going though a period of reform as they look to gain admission to the EU. Parliment in 1999, with an overwhelming majority, for the removal of military judges in State Security Courts. In October 2001, Parliament voted for 34 changes to the Constitution, including the abolition of the death penalty except in times of war and for acts of terrorism, ending the practice of torture, and allowing the use of the Kurdish language in broadcasting and education.


Turkeys HDI is 0.767 as of 2016, the UK has a HDI of 0.909 and the USA has one of 0.920. Turkey ranks 71 overall, just two ranks below Iran. In Turkey a person can be expected to have 14.6 years of schooling, and live to the age of 75.5, around 4 years shorter than the US life span, and 5 years longer than Russia. Turkeys Gini coefficient is 40.18, just lower than 41.06 in the USA and Nigerias score of 42.97.
