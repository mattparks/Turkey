The Republic of Turkey has a Consitution establishing a Republican Parliamentary Democracy, and the current constitution was adopted in 1982 and is written in a Westen style. The judicial branch has judicial review, judges are appointed by the president and have to be of age 65. The executive branch is controled like by a president. The president has a limited direct influence in policy making, but as head the of the executive they control a large beurocracy like in the US. The parliament is controled by a prime ministor who is appointed by the president, they also supervises the Council of Ministers. There is a cabinet (Council of Ministers) that is controled by both the PM and President.
