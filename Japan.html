Institutions

Executive

  • PM and Ministers he/she appoints
  • PM appointed by Emperor after nomination by Diet
  • Responsible for creating policy initiatives and driving nations ideas

Legislative

  • Bicameral --> Diet consisting of House of Legislators and House of Councilors
  • Emperor 'presides' over Diet
  • Responsible for making laws and checking the PM's powers

Judicial

  • Independent Judiciary
  • Has Supreme Court with Judicial Review
  • Has other levels of courts as well

History

Until 1868

  • Was dominated by various monarchies in the past, until country rose to empire status
  • Kings became emperors, and began a mission to expand the country westward
  • Politically authoritarian, Economically neoliberal

World War II

  • Throughout the war was increasingly cruel to prisoners and territories they captured
  • Lost may soldiers and in the end, lost the war
  • After, US occupation until 1952 to implement liberal democratic institutions and setup into the country
  • Politically authoritarian, increasingly economically Keynesian during WW2

Modern Day

  • Emperor still exists, but has no real political power
  • Now a liberal democracy based off of the styles of America and Great Britain
  • Politically liberal,Economically liberal

Modern Politics

Economy

  • Neoliberal economy because of participation in trade deals with US and other western countries
  • 1.4% Agricultural
  • Major Exports: Motor Vehicles, electronics, machine tools, steel

Legitimacy

  • Rational-legal legitimacy
  • Extremely legitimate because of free and fair elections, guarenteed civil rights and liberties, and representative political parties

Political Culture

  • Liberal-Democratic is the conservative party, supported by older population
  • Democratic party is the second largest party, supported by younger citizens
  • Family has a large influence on political socialization along with education, friends, etc
  • Youth becoming more and more liberal in comparison to parents, more likely to support Democratic Party

Domestic Policy

  • Economic reforms are a major concern with rising deficit spending in Japan
  • Health care is public and affordable
  • Elementary and Secondary education required, high school and above not required

Cleavages

  • Mostly culturally and religiously unified
  • Age: Many people younger & not having children

Political Parties

  • Liberal-Democrats
    • Conservative Political Party
    • Near total control of government since 1955 likely due to culturally unified Japanese
    • Wins legitimate free & fair elections
    • Founded in 1955
    • Very western, promotes free trade and working with United States
  • Democrats
    • More liberal Political Party
    • Main opposition party
  • Many other smaller parties

Election System - Mixed system, combines elements of FPTP and PR

Current Results

  • House of Representatives
    • LDP obtained less than 50% of vote, coalized with Komeito to get 50%
    • Democratic Party was next highest party with 22.51%
  • House of Councillors
    • LDP Coalized with NKP to obtain necessary seats
    • Next highest party was Democratic Party with 16.3% of vote
  • Expected to stay on par in the future with USA and other developed countries in democratization

Foreign Relations

  • Neoliberal trade relationship with most other countries, especially western ones
  • Member of United Nations
  • Enemies with China
  • Treaty of mutual cooperation and security signed with US in 1960, US founded Japan's current government, so US relationship is positive
